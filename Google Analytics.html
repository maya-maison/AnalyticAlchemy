Digital Marketing Performance Optimization: Analyzing Google Merchandise Store

In this project, I conducted an in-depth analysis of Google Analytics data from Google's official merchandise store (shop.merch.google). The goal was to uncover insights about audience behavior, traffic sources, and conversion patterns to drive business results and optimize marketing performance.

Problem and Approach

The challenge was to analyze Google Analytics data from the Google Merchandise Store to optimize digital marketing performance and drive business results. My approach involved:

  1. Setting Key Business Objectives:

    I defined SMART objectives focused on increasing user count, improving user stickiness, and boosting average purchase revenue per user.

  2. Identifying Key Performance Indicators (KPIs):

    I selected relevant KPIs to measure progress towards each business objective, such as email marketing metrics, DAU/MAU stickiness rate, and average purchase revenue per user.

  3. A/B Testing Proposal:

    I developed an A/B testing plan to improve email open rates by personalizing subject lines, demonstrating the ability to design and interpret controlled experiments.

  4. Data Exploration and Segmentation:

    I analyzed various Google Analytics reports to uncover insights about user behavior, acquisition channels, and revenue patterns.

Key Findings

Through this analysis, I uncovered several significant insights:

  1. Seasonal Trends:

    Identified September 2023 as the month with the highest new user count and January 2024 as the lowest, potentially due to post-holiday purchasing patterns.

  2. Channel Performance:

    Discovered that the Direct channel generated the highest total revenue, while channels like Paid Shopping showed high engagement but low user acquisition.

  3. Campaign Effectiveness:

    Analyzed ROAS for various campaigns, identifying opportunities to reallocate budget from underperforming campaigns to those with higher returns.

  4. eCommerce Improvements:

    Suggested UX enhancements such as adding customer reviews and personalized product recommendations to improve conversion rates.

Data Source

The data used for this analysis came from Google Analytics for the Google Merchandise Store (shop.merch.google).
