NYSE Data Analysis: Extracting Financial Insights

In this project, I was tasked with analyzing a comprehensive NYSE dataset to uncover financial trends and patterns across various sectors and industries. The challenge was to transform raw financial data into actionable insights that could inform strategic decision-making.

Problem and Approach

I approached this complex dataset by first structuring and enhancing the raw data, then applying statistical analyses and creating dynamic financial models. My process involved:

  1. Data Preparation:

    I transformed the raw NYSE data into a structured format, calculating crucial financial metrics like Operating Income, Gross Margin, and Operating Margin. Additionally, I incorporated industry group, industry, and sub-industry levels of the GICS classification to provide more granular insights and enable a deeper understanding of financial trends across various segments.

  2. Statistical Analysis:

    After formatting and transforming the raw data, I examined measures of center (mean, median) and measures of spread (standard deviation, range) to gain a deeper understanding of the distribution of financial metrics. This analysis helped to identify central tendencies and the variability within each GICS classification, revealing significant patterns and outliers.



  1. Visualization:

    I created various data visualizations to effectively communicate patterns and trends.

  2. Financial Modeling:

    I developed a dynamic Profit and Loss statement and a forecasting model with multiple scenarios.



Key Findings

Through this analysis, I uncovered several significant insights:

  1. Sectoral Variability:

    I identified notable standard deviation peaks in sectors such as Food & Staples Retailing, Oil, Gas, & Consumable Fuels, and Technology Hardware, Storage & Peripherals.

  2. Revenue Trends:

    A sharp decline in revenues was detected in 2015, relative to the number of ticker symbols, which upon further investigation was largely attributed to the Energy sector.



  1. Market Patterns:

    I unearthed temporal trends spanning 2003-2017, with heightened activity in 2014-2016, notably marked by a bear market crash in 2015.

Data Source

The dataset used for this analysis was the SEC 10K annual filings (2016-2012) from Kaggle, containing fundamental financial data for 500 companies listed on the New York Stock Exchange.
